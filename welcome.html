TRAVELING.... AND ARIZONA

Basically, I really like traveling. It's been super rainy the past few days so I didn't feel inspired so take many photos but over the last year, I traveled to a lot of awesome spots and I used images from those trips to create this web page. I became very complacent and depressed after working at Chrysler for five years right out of high-school... so I quit. I used money I had saved over my time working their and spent the year taking different trips, to visit family and go hiking.

Below is a photo that I took in Arizona, at Devil's Bridge. Devil's Bridge I would rate as a medium difficulty hike that is out and back 4.2 miles. The hike leads to an amazing look out point of some beatiful red rocks in Sedona. Initially I went to Arizona because I had planned to hike Havasupai Falls and realized I was DRAMATICALL unprepared for that hike. Sedona wasn't on my list of places to visit during that trip but I ended up spending several days there because I fell in love with it.

I spent alot of time in California growing up, so I was use to seeing mountains but I had never seen mountains like that before. The way the sun hits the red rocks is so breath taking. While I was in Sedona I learn about the Sedonda Vortices... yes, I am into spirtual woo-woo B.S. and metaphysical stuff. Sedona is considered have "vortex energy". Vortices are places where the earth seems especially alive with energy. Many people feel inspired, recharged or uplifted after visiting a vortex. If you've ever been to Sedona, I think it's hard to deny that you feel SOMETHING different energetically there.

Below, is a video I took on my first "long" back-packing trip. I went with a friend I've know since high school. This video is a little embarassing to include but it is the first "vlog" I made... I used only my photo to edit and shoot it and I had a lot of fun editing it.

The trail we hiked was Eagle Rock Loop in Ouchita National Forest... it's roughly a 30 mile trail where you actually hike 5 different mountain peaks. I didn't realize that before commiting to the hike it was one of the most difficult loop trails in the United States. I thought I was going to die several times during the hike... after I got home, I lost 4 differnt toe nails. Anyways.... just watch the video.
